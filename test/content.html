The 30 Best Summer Movies of All-Time, Ranked

  • Summer 2015 is almost over, and it's become one of the biggest ever at the box office. In honor of our favorite movie season winding down, we ranked the 30 best summer blockbusters of all-time. From "Star Wars" to "Lion King," we got all your popcorn entertainment needs covered.

  • 30. 'There's Something About Mary' (1998)

    Release Date: July 15, 1998

    Almost 20 years since it's release, the "hair gel" scene gets us every time. The Farrelly Brothers' hit movie paved the way for future raunchy, R-rated comedies -- almost a decade before Judd Apatow. "Mary" also turned Ben Stiller and Cameron Diaz into household names. Lots of repeat business came from this Summer of '98 entry, and it still holds up (pretty well) today.

  • 29. 'Grease' (1978)

    Release Date: June 1, 1978

    Aaaaand now we have "Summer Nights" and all the songs stuck in our heads. If there is a more timeless musical out there about the '50s, we don't wanna know about it.

  • 28. 'Ghost' (1990)

    Release Date: July 13, 1990

    The biggest hit of Summer 1990, so big you'd be a pariah among friends if you hadn't seen "Ghost" at least thrice in theaters. The love story between Sam (Patrick Swayze) and Molly (a never-better Demi Moore) is full of thrills, laughs and tears; a delicate mix that should never work on paper, let alone so well on the big screen. Whoopi Goldberg's Oscar-winning performance as the medium helping Sam's struggling spirit still holds up 25 years later.

  • 27. 'Guardians of the Galaxy' (2014)

    Release Date: August 1, 2014

    "What a bunch of a-holes." Marvel bet big on a movie starring Chris Pratt, a talking tree and sentient, gun-toting raccoon -- and so did audiences. "Guardians" became the movie of Summer 2014, the rare blockbuster to earn that which so few movies can these days: Repeat viewings, in the theater. The next installment of Marvel's unlikely franchise can't get here fast enough.

  • 26. 'Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy' (2004)

    Release Date: July 9, 2004

    Endlessly quotable and scary funny, the world of Ron Burgundy is full of polyester suits and lamp-loving weathermen. Will Ferrell commits so fully here, the character has become his most iconic one. While it wasn't an incredibly huge hit at the box office, you couldn't go anywhere during the Summer of 2004 without hearing your friend's impersonation of Ron.

  • 25. 'Speed' (1994)

    Release Date: June 10, 1994

    "Speed" was the little bomb-on-a-bus thriller that could, proving to be a more popular hit with audiences and critics than Fox's other blockbuster that summer, James Cameron's "True Lies." Uncredited script doctor Joss Whedon gave Jon de Bont's first feature film a strong dose of humor and heart, turning Keanu Reeves into an action hero and Sandra Bullock into, well, Sandra Bullock. Stop fighting the urge to rewatch this now. It's ok, go -- we're right behind ya.

  • 24. 'Toy Story 3' (2010)

    Release Date: June 18, 2010

    Holy s--t, that incinerator scene?! That tearful moment was worth the 11-year wait for this threequel, which proves that not all Part 3's are meh. Woody, Buzz and the gang struggle with abandonment issues when an off-to-college Andy moves on from his toys. What they find after being donated to a daycare center is a renewed sense of purpose, a new owner and one more chance for Andy to play with them. And no, we're not crying again. That's just -- someone must be chopping onions.

  • 23. 'Spider-Man' (2002)

    Release Date: May 3, 2002

    The biggest movie of Summer 2002, "Spider-Man" beat expectations -- and "Attack of the Clones" -- at the box office, grossing over $400 million and playing in theaters until late summer. The film's staying power is a testament to director Sam Raimi's deft handling of the story's set pieces and emotional core, as well as the appeal of Tobey Maguire as Peter Parker. In a post-"Dark Knight" world, "Spider-Man" seems a bit camp in sections. But attention must be paid to the Webhead's first big-screen success.

  • 22. 'The Avengers' (2012)

    Release Date: May 4, 2012

    It's not a perfect movie, but it is a great time at the movies. Joss Whedon managed to effortlessly pull off uniting Earth's Mightiest Heroes for the first time on the big screen, while creating a movie with tons of repeat viewing value. "Avengers" changed the way we make comic book movies -- and inspired studios, for better or worse, to raid their slates for shared universe opportunities. We'll be feeling this one's ripple effects for a long time to come.

  • 21. 'Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade' (1989)

    Release Date: May 24, 1989

    Summer '89 gave us an embarrassment of blockbuster riches: "Batman," Lethal Weapon 2," and "Last Crusade," one of the best Indy adventures ever. The film's rewatchability is in large part due to the chemistry between Indy and his dad, played memorably by Sean Connery. It's too bad they never got around to making that fourth film (wink wink)...

  • 20. 'Mad Max: Fury Road' (2015)

    Release Date: May 15, 2015

    Think it's too early to crown George Miller's masterpiece as "all-time" worthy? Well that attitude's not gonna get you carried through the gates of Valhalla. It's more than a well-made, relentless car chase of a movie. "Fury Road" is world-building at its most effortless and infectious; it finds the exact amount of whatever emotional drama the story needs to service without ever feeling secondary to the action. Moreover, it will be the the film everyone looks back on in 10, 15 years with the praise we're giving it now. Because that's just how we roll.

  • 19. 'Finding Nemo' (2003)

    Release Date: May 30, 2003

    One of Pixar's biggest hits is still one of their best films. The stunning underwater animation, coupled with the very simple, very identifiable tale of a widowed father desperate to track down his missing son, resonates long after the end credits roll. Let's hope the magic is still there when "Finding Dory" hits theaters in 2016.

  • 18. 'Spider-Man 2' (2004)

    Release Date: June 30, 2004

    Sam Raimi’s hit sequel doubles-down on its hero never catching a break, resulting in an inventive escalation of stakes and tension for everyone’s favorite wallcrawler. The exciting set pieces are bolstered by then-benchmark setting CG, as Peter Parker struggles with who he is versus who he wants to be -- in between kick-punching Doc Ock. Before “Dark Knight” came along, this was considered the best comic book movie ever. For some fans, it still is.

  • 17. 'Gladiator' (2000)

    Release Date: May 5, 2000

    Ridley Scott's Oscar-winning epic sold a lot of tickets in May 2000, riding decent reviews and excellent box office to earn it Best Picture status. It turned Russell Crowe into a (then) A-list star and broke Scott out of Director Jail. While the filmmaker has been hit-or-miss in the 15 years since Maximus asked "Are we not entertained?!", there is no denying "Gladiator's" place as engaging summer movie entertainment.

  • 16. 'Top Gun' (1986)

    Release Date: May 16, 1986

    The need for speed? Check. An infectious Kenny Loggins soundtrack? Check. Slow-mo, shirtless homoerotic volleyball scenes? Check AND mate! "Top Gun" forged a leading man out of Tom Cruise, and audiences fell for his cocky Maverick and the movie's 'Merica values and incredible (and all practical) aerial dogfight action. Almost 30 years later, we still cry when Goose dies and swoon when we hear the Righteous Brothers.

  • 15. 'Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan' (1982)

    Release Date: June 4, 1982

    The best "Star Trek" movie ever made, this sequel to the classic series episode "Space Seed" pits Admiral Kirk against a revenge-fueled Khan and lots of space battle goodness ensues. In between the phaser fire, "Khan" delivers the most emotionally resonate entry in the series, and for the genre.

  • 14. 'Independence Day' (1996)

    Release Date: July 3, 1996

    One of the first modern blockbusters to have a Tuesday night release (a big deal in 1996), "Independence Day" is a modern classic of ridiculous summer blockbuster filmmaking. It turned blowing up landmarks into a genre staple, reigniting Hollywood's enthusiasm for disaster movies (thank "ID4" for "Armageddon," kids). It also proved that Will Smith was a movie star in waiting. And you guys re-watch it every July 4th too, amirites?!

  • 13. 'Batman' (1989)

    Release Date: June 23, 1989

    Before "Batman," there wasn't quite a blockbuster like it. If you didn't see the movie at least three times in theaters, you were doing something wrong. Audiences lined-up to see Michael Keaton's Dark Knight battle Nicholson's iconic Joker in the first "dark and gritty" comic book adaptation that would pave the way for decades of DC and Marvel movies. While Nolan may have done Batman best, Tim Burton did it first.

  • 12. 'The Lion King (1994)

    Release Date: June 15, 1994

    A bedrock of many a childhood, Disney's classic movie -- you know what? Nevermind. You know how good this movie is. Instead of us reminding you, how 'bout we just go watch it again instead. Cool? Cool.

  • 11. 'Terminator 2: Judgment Day' (1991)

    Release Date: July 3, 1991

    While the first film has the tighter pacing and screenplay, "T2" is an action-packed benchmark that builds upon the first film's foundation to deliver the best sequel the franchise has ever produced. (Sorry, not sorry, "Genisys"). It made CG the tool of modern moviemaking; without it we wouldn't have "Jurassic Park."

  • 10. 'Jurassic Park' (1993)

    Release Date: June 11, 1993

    That picture pretty much says it all. Thanks to Steven Spielberg and Michael Crichton, we can't think of dinosaurs without instantly associating it with "Jurassic Park."

  • 9. 'The Dark Knight' (2008)

    Release Date: July 18, 2008

    Christopher Nolan goes bigger and bolder for this sequel to "Batman Begins," which introduced a gripping new take on Joker (played magnificently by the late Heath Ledger). It also follows the trinity of Batman, Commissioner Gordon and Harvey Dent through their tragically-doomed crusade to save Gotham.

  • 8. 'Aliens' (1986)

    Release Date: July 18, 1986

    Ellen Ripley takes on the Alien Queen in this sci-fi classic from writer-director James Cameron, who goes the pure action-horror route with this sequel to Ridley Scott's "Alien." Like the "Terminator" films, "Aliens" also sets a very high bar for both the genre and the summer movie blockbuster.

  • 7. 'Die Hard' (1988)

    Release Date: July 15, 1988

    Legions of action movies owe their existence to "Die Hard," the best R-rated Christmas movie ever made. Audiences discovered this in the Summer of 1988, as the film turned into a sleeper hit on its way to become a classic. Three of the "Die Hard" sequels were also summer releases, but none of them come close to John McClane's first "wrong place, wrong time" outing.

  • 6. 'E.T.' / 'The Empire Strikes Back' (1982, 1980) - Tie

    Release Dates: June 11, 1982 ('E.T.'), May 21, 1980 ('Empire')

    It's a Sophie's Choice for us between these two iconic blockbusters. Spielberg gets us right in the feels with his "boy meets alien" adventure that finds the fantastic even in suburbia. And "Empire Strikes Back" is -- well, there's just life before and life after "Luke, I am your father."

  • 5. 'Ghostbusters' (1984)

    Release Date: June 30, 1984

    Four guys in jump suits, who believe in "spooks, specters and ghosts," power this sci-fi comedy classic, which reigned for several weeks atop the box office in '84. Here's hoping the 2016 movie with an all-female cast of Ghostbusters is just as beloved (and funny) as this one.

  • 4. 'Back to the Future' (1985)

    Release Date: July 3, 1985

    For just turning 30, "BTTF" hasn't aged a day in terms of entertainment value. Audiences couldn't get enough of Marty McFly and Doc Brown's time-traveling misadventures, and they still can't. "Heavy," indeed.

  • 3. 'Raiders of the Lost Ark' (1981)

    Release Date: June 1, 1981

    Do yourself a favor. If you haven't seen Indy's first (and arguably best) adventure in a long time, pop in the Blu-ray. Or, even better? Find yourself the biggest screen possible to experience Spielberg's most polished summer blockbuster.

  • 2. 'Jaws' (1975)

    Release Date: June 20, 1975

    Speaking of Spielberg, his "Jaws" is largely responsible for why people make lists like this one. The first blockbuster ever made should serve as a template for all that came after: Intense thrills and spectacle all told at human height. Spielberg grounds his shark-hunting thriller on the backs of very relatable, very likable characters -- every victory they have or panicked breath they take feels like one of our own. So no surprise that, after 40 years, we're still afraid to go in the water.

  • 1. 'Star Wars' (1977)

    Release Date: May 25, 1977

    You were expecting something else? No movie changed a culture, or continue to do so, like "Star Wars." People lined up for hours to see the film in the Summer of '77, and they are eager to do it again for "Force Awakens." If only more modern summer product could capture why we go to the movies as well as our first trip to that galaxy far, far away...
