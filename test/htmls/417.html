Top 10 Teen Drama TV Series On Netflix: Gossip Girl, Gilmore Girls, Pretty Little Liars And More

For our weekly Netflix round-up, we’ve ranked the top 10 teen TV dramas available on instant streaming. Whether you’re more “Gossip Girl” or “Gilmore Girls,” you’ll find something to love here:



(Photo : Secret Life of the American Teenager)



10. The Secret Life Of The American Teenager
 This ABC Family drama put Shailene Woodley on the map as pregnant fifteen-year-old Amy Juergens. There are plenty of breakups, makeups, love triangles and multiple pregnancies.



(Photo : The Vampire Diaries)



9. The Vampire Diaries
 This mega-popular CW series will continue into its seventh season later this year, far outlasting the “Twilight” vampire craze that sparked it. The supernatural drama revolves around a love triangle between human Elena (Nina Dobrev) and vampire brothers Stefan (Paul Wesley) and Damon (Ian Somerhalder).



(Photo : Pretty Little Liars)




8. Pretty Little Liars
 ABC Family series “Pretty Little Liars” is currently airing its sixth season. The show follows four girls - Aria, Hanna, Emily and Spencer - as their clique is tormented by the mysterious “A.”



(Photo : One Tree Hill)



7. One Tree Hill
 This 9-season series is a teen drama classic! It sparked the careers of Chad Michael Murray, Sophia Bush and caused plenty of on-set drama as well. The series follows two half-brothers as they go through high school and into young adulthood.



(Photo : Friday Night Lights)



6. Friday Night Lights
 This drama series about a high school football team in a small town in Texas was critically acclaimed and featured an all-star cast including Kyle Chandler, Connie Britton, Minka Kelly, Adrianne Palicki, Taylor Kitsch and Michael B. Jordan.



(Photo : Gossip Girl)



5. Gossip Girl
 XOXO. This six-season CW series follows the scandalous lives of Upper East Side teenagers in prestigious private schools (and later, Ivy League colleges). It launched the careers of Blake Lively, Leighton Meester, Penn Badgley and others.



(Photo : Gilmore Girls)



4. Gilmore Girls
 This witty dramedy follows mother/daughter duo Lorelai and Rory and Rory goes through the prestigious private school Chilton and on to Yale. It launched the careers of Lauren Graham, Alexis Bledel and Melissa McCarthy.



(Photo : Buffy the Vampire Slayer)



3. Buffy The Vampire Slayer
 This supernatural teen drama stars Sarah Michelle Gellar as a high school student who fights vampires as a chosen Slayer. The series has become iconic and launched the careers of many of the stars and creator Joss Whedon.



(Photo : Skins)



2. Skins
 This British teen series has been praised for its unflinching look at mental illness, drug use, sexuality and death. Unusually for a teen drama, it replaced its cast every two years. Notable cast members include Nicholas Hoult, Dev Patel, Jack O’Connell and Kaya Scodelario.



(Photo : Freaks and Geeks)



1. Freaks And Geeks
 This one-season dramedy was directed by Judd Apatow and aired for 18 episodes in 1999 and 2000. It has since become a cult hit and featured stars including James Franco, Jason Segel, Seth Rogen and Linda Cardinelli.
