EAT grows appetite with analytics transformation


By Strahan Wilson

- Chief financial officer at EAT
7 Apr, 2015 09:03 am
3

comment(s)

As a cafe chain that has grown quickly to have over 100 outlets across the UK, EAT began to struggle with our laborious spreadsheet planning which no longer suited our scale. A move to cloud-based planning and analytics is empowering us with the right information, as we attempt to double our business size in three years.


By the end of 2013, as our business reached large scale, we began to drown in hundreds of different active cells across three key planning spreadsheets. We knew we had to change our methods of forecasting at different levels, and how we predict the future.

As with many businesses, having used a basic spreadsheet for finance for many years, we needed a more adaptable and scalable system, that would automatically update when we changed any assumptions for the future of our revenue and profit, or changed store level data.

We operate several different spreadsheets, at the store planning level, the overall company level, and the three-year future plan. In the past, by using spreadsheets finance staff had to either update one spreadsheet and then each of the others accordingly, a very lengthy process, or leave out changes from future predictions. This meant that essentially we had poor and limited insight into the future of our business.

Understanding a billion figures

By February 2014, we had decided it was necessary to move to a more sophisticated, cloud-based planning tool that will allow us to easily enter data, and quickly produce reports and analysis. The new system has been fully integrated, allowing us to process data at all of the different levels and to automatically change any methodology or assumptions across the different planning criteria.

The effect has been immense. We now process over one billion cells of data. On a spreadsheet of this size, it would be almost impossible to view on a computer, let alone actually use, update or understand.

Our cloud-based reporting allows us to more easily reconcile figures. It also gives us proper insight into different stores' performance, and on company issues such as finance, marketing and supply. It will empower us to bring about our three-year plan by proper modelling.

Enabling us to grow

Given the improvement in the UK economy, as well as a boom in the cafe sector as fewer people bring packed lunches to work, we are fully confident we can achieve a doubling of our business in three years. Having the right figures to hand, and the right predictions, is essential to this - it enables us not only to plan properly but also to provide the necessary future prediction data to banks.

Operating the system in the cloud also gives us the scalability to vastly increase our data and analytics capabilities as we grow, without having to increase our infrastructure.

On a day-to-day level, the system helps us to understand our business much better. We can create a set of downloads to run back into our spreadsheets, providing a simple chart of figures measured against key performance indicators. It enables us to make decisions within finance, and to take figures to marketing, sales and the supply chain to better inform planning.

We have great plans for the platform as we expand our company. At the moment, it is available only to finance, though of course we can access all the different tranches of data. We expect to expand it to be a self-service platform for many different areas of our business. This will enable them to extract their own data and produce reports, while eliminating error and preparing our business for the future.
