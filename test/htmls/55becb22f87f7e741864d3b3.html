25 comic books for nerdy newbies to read first

3.0k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Andrea Romano2015-08-02 14:00:19 UTC

Every comic book nerd has an origin story, and it starts with opening that first book.

There are so many titles with so much history, so it's natural for a new comic book reader to be intimidated by jumping into the fandom. When it comes to picking up comics with rich backgrounds, it can kind of feel like starting a movie half way through.

See also: 20 Comics for Non-Comic Book Readers

Mashable consulted with Sam Maggs, associate editor of The Mary Sue, to give her best advice for the newbies out there. "Try not to feel overwhelmed. There is so much fandom out there, and that’s beyond making your way through the entirety of the text," she said.

Maggs' best advice to the interested nerds out there is to start slow by dipping your toes into the community itself by following fan blogs, writers and artists. "From there you can start commenting, making friends and maybe contributing to the fan community with your own original works, too! But don’t worry if you can’t do it all at once -– the fans are going to be waiting for you when you’re ready."

Most importantly, Maggs encourages all fans to be themselves. "Don't pretend to be someone you’re not, to like things or people you don’t, in the hopes that the people around you will like you -– you’ll just make yourself miserable."

Luckily, there are plenty of titles that don't require a library's worth of prior knowledge that you can start reading while you're exploring the comic book world — including some that Maggs recommends herself.

Comics for Newbies

  1. 1. Ms. Marvel by G. Willow Wilson and Adrian Alphona

    The latest reboot of Ms. Marvel says goodbye to the classic Carol Danvers for Kamala Khan, an ordinary New Jersey girl who is gifted with extraordinary powers.

    Image: Amazon Marvel

  2. 2. The Runaways by Brian K. Vaughan, Adrian Alphona and Takeshi Miyazawa

    A lot of teens suspect their parents are supervillains, but these six kids from Los Angeles are actually right. While discovering their superpowers, they band together to become heroes rather than following in their parents' footsteps.

    Image: Amazon Marvel

  3. 3. Saga by Brian K. Vaughan and Fiona Staples

    This book has everything: intergalactic war, star-crossed lovers, high tech, magic -- and all told through a narrator who is a baby for most of the story.

    Image: Amazon Image Comics

  4. 4. The Wicked & The Divine by Kieron Gillen, Jamie McKelvie and Matt Wilson

    Pagan gods become pop culture icons in this publication from Image Comics. Of course, just because these characters are gods doesn't mean they're perfect.

    Image: Amazon Image Comics

  5. 5. Young Avengers by Kieron Gillen and Jamie McKelvie

    No more Captain America and his crew. Instead teens superheroes Wiccan, Hulkling, Kate "Hawkeye" Bishop, Kid Loki, Marvel Boy and Ms. America take up the Avengers mantle.

    Image: Amazon Marvel

  6. 6. Flight by Kazu Kibuishi

    Kazu Kibuishi compiles beautiful stories about flight and flying from 21 different, young comic book authors.

    Image: Amazon Villard

  7. 7. Y: The Last Man by Brian K. Vaughan and Pia Guerra

    This dystopian graphic novels follows the assumed only man on Earth after all the other male mammals have died. Get ready to hunker down for it, though; there are 60 issues in the series.

    Image: Amazon Vertigo

  8. 8. Batgirl by Brenden Fletcher, Cameron Stewart and Babs Tarr

    Batgirl joins the line up of classic superheroes who have been revamped for the new generation. In this publication, Batgirl is a crime fighter who also lives with a roommate in Burnside, a hip, Williamsburg-inspired neighborhood.

    Image: Amazon DC Comics

  9. 9. Sandman by Neil Gaiman, Sam Keith and Mike Dringenberg

    Gaiman combines ancient myth and fairytales to tell the story of Dream -- literally, the lord of dreams -- who attempts to recover sacred objects stolen from him.

    Image: Amazon Sandman

  10. 10. Batman: The Long Halloween by Jeph Loeb and Tim Sale

    This short Batman series is perfect for the new reader. It follows the Dark Knight in his early crime-fighting days as he investigates a mysterious murder.

    Image: Amazon DC Comics

  11. 11. Lumberjanes by Noelle Stevenson, Grace Ellis, Brooke Allen and Shannon Watters

    A group of Lumberjane Scouts solve mysteries around the Miss Quinzella Thiskwin Penniquiqul Thistle Crumpet's Camp for Hardcore Lady Types in this book from Image Comics.

    Image: Amazon BOOM! Box

  12. 12. The Walking Dead by Robert Kirkman and Tony Moore

    Rick Grimes and his family (including other survivors) battle zombies in this post-apocalyptic story. Plus, there is way more juicy information in the book than is included in the TV show.

    Image: Amazon Image Comics

  13. 13. Birds of Prey by Duane Swierczynski

    Penned by some of the best comic writers from Chuck Dixon to Gail Simone, this break off story stars a band of kick-ass superwomen such as Black Canary, Batgirl, Huntress and Hawkgirl.

    Image: Amazon DC Comics

  14. 14. Static Shock by Dwayne McDuffie, Robert L. Washington III and John Paul Leon

    Virgil Hawkins, also known as the superhero Static, is just a regular high school student who happens to have incredible powers. He juggles school and being a vigilante in his neighborhood.

    Image: Amazon DC Comics

  15. 15. Fables by Bill Willingham, James Jean and Alex Maleev

    If you're feeling brave, Fables has a rich story -- 149 issues worth. The story follows fairytale people who have been displaced by a supervillain.

    Image: Amazon Vertigo

  16. 16. Super Mutant Magic Academy by Jillian Tamaki

    The Super Mutant Magic Academy is a prep school for witches and mutants. This former strip comic combines plenty of wit and high school drama for a truly enjoyable read.

    Image: Amazon Drawn and Quarterly

  17. 17. Captain Marvel by Kelly Sue Deconnick and David Lopez

    Former Ms. Marvel, Carol Danvers, is in a new role as Captain Marvel in this somewhat new publication. Carol saves the world and embarks on a new life as an even mightier superhero.

    Image: Amazon Marvel

  18. 18. Clumsy by Jeffrey Brown

    This graphic novel shows the roller coaster of emotions that happen when you're in a long distance relationship.

    Image: Amazon Top Shelf Productions

  19. 19. Scott Pilgrim by Bryan Lee O'Malley

    Watch Scott fight his girlfriend's evil exes in this quirky publication that inspired the movie starring Michael Cera.

    Image: Amazon Oni Press

  20. 20. Spider-man: Blue by Jeph Loeb and Tim Sale

    This one-off series explores Peter Parker and how he fell in love with Gwen Stacy.

    Image: Amazon Marvel

  21. 21. Superman: Birthright by Mark Waid, Leinil Francis Yu, Gerry Alanguilan and Dave McCaig

    This publication takes you back to the beginning of Superman's origin story -- back when he was a simple farm boy/alien in Kansas.

    Image: Amazon DC Comics

  22. 22. Batwoman: Elegy by Greg Rucka and J.H. Williams III

    Batwoman battles madwoman Alice (as in Alice in Wonderland) to save Gotham City in this short story arc.

    Image: Amazon DC Comics

  23. 23. The Watchmen by Alan Moore and Dave Gibbons

    This title has been hailed as one of the greatest of all time -- so any comic book fan should get a taste of it. The story follows flawed superheroes as they are stalked by an assassin.

    Image: Amazon DC Comics

  24. 24. Sex Criminals by Matt Fraction and Chip Zdarsky

    This quirky series is all about Suzie, a woman who can stop time when she has sex. When she meets a guy, Jon, with the same ability, they find manage to use their powers to, of course, rob banks.

    Image: Amazon Image Comics

  25. 25. Ultimate X-Men by Mark Millar and Geoff Johns

    Before you start cowering at the thought of starting with the detailed world of X-Men as a newbie, rest assured that this modernized title has quicker plots and less need for knowledge of the X-Men universe -- which you can definitely dive into if you're looking to be a Marvel expert. It's still a pretty long read, with 100 issues.

    Image: Amazon Marvel

Maggs is also the author of The Fangirl's Guide to the Galaxy: A Handbook for Girl Geeks, which provides plenty of handy tips for every kind of fan, in any fandom, from comic books to Battlestar Galactica.
