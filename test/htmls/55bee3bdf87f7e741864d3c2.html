Softbank's Pepper robot offered as rental assistant for Japan businesses

1.8k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Adario Strange2015-08-03 01:30:12 UTC

When Softbank made its Pepper helper robot available for sale back in June, the device sold out almost immediately.

Now, before announcing the availability of additional units to the general public, the company is offering the robot as a rental device, officially putting robots in the same category as other mainstream rental appliances (at least in Japan).

See also: Japan's NTT to put home care robot's in the homes of the elderly

The price for the "Pepper for Biz" service will be 55,000 yen per month, or about $444. (Incidentally, the number four is considered an unlucky number in Japan. Kind of like the number 13 in the U.S. But that's all silly robot apocalypse superstition, right?) Users will be required to sign a three-year contract, which will total about $16,000 in rental charges.

Softbank's business version of Pepper will come with pre-installed applications designed to allow businesses to use the robot as an automated receptionist and as a means to engage potential new customers in public spaces.

If anything goes wrong with the robot, users will be able to get support either online or via telephone. That support will also include the ability to request repairs to the robot and, if necessary, the option to order a replacement robot.

So far, the robot is only available in Japan, but Softbank founder Masayoshi Son hopes to offer the robot outside of Japan next year. Pre-orders for the Pepper for Biz robot will be available starting October 1.
