The Best Hotels for Your Next Staycation, from New York City to Los Angeles

July 20, 2015 9:30 am by Christina Liao
Expand

Photographed by Peter Lindbergh, Vogue, July 2015

It’s Monday night at 11:00 p.m. and you’re scrolling through Instagram. One person is jumping off a yacht into the crystal-clear waters of Greece, another has taken a tide-fall selfie at McWay Falls in Big Sur. FOMO hits. But the thought of going through the motions required to book a vacation are unfathomable: looking up flights, searching for a place to stay in parts unknown, devising an itinerary. Why not take a staycation—with five-star amenities? Here, seven hotels that will prove that staying local has never been more appealing.

1 / 7
Terranea

If you live in: Los Angeles (about 45 minutes away)

What to do: The 102-acre, peninsula-set Terranea offers a spa that spans 50,000 square feet, offering treatment rooms with panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean, peaceful sauna rooms, and an oceanfront yoga and pilates studio. If you decide to forgo the spa pool, lounge around at the adult-only Cielo Point with a drink in hand. And for the active type, golfing, paddleboarding, kayorkling (yes, that would be kayaking and snorkling combined), horseback riding, helicopter tours of the peninsula, and even falconry are all options. For nourishment, there are eight dining establishments to choose from, but don't pass on Mar'sel, the waterfront farm-to-table restaurant that uses locally grown produce and herbs gathered from a garden just steps away from the kitchen.

The splurge: While even the standard rooms offer a private patio or balcony, go with the residential-style, oceanfront Bungalow, unwind by either the indoor or outdoor fireplace, and enjoy the benefits of complimentary entry to the spa pool and its facilities, breakfast and lunch at the café, and discounts on spa treatments.

Rates: Rooms start from $395 per night; Bungalows start from $1,100 per night
Photo: Courtesy of Terranea
2 / 7
Soho House New York

If you live in: New York City

What to do: There’s a reason this spot is a perennial favorite. It has one of the amenities most sought after by New Yorkers: a rooftop pool. Yes, it's an exclusive club, but the hotel is available to nonmembers, and guests have access to all of the same niceties. Sip on the refreshing cucumber-based Eastern Standard cocktail poolside, relax at the Cowshed Spa, enjoy American Italian food alfresco, and watch a film in the Screening Room.

The splurge: Pass on the Small room and go for at least a Medium so you can treat yourself to a soothing bath with Cowshed bubbles.

Rates: Small rooms start from $485 per night, Medium rooms from $625, and Big rooms from $970
Photo: Courtesy of Soho House
3 / 7
The Miami Beach Edition

If you live in: Miami or Fort Lauderdale (about 45 minutes away)

What to do: The Miami Beach Edition is the city’s newest draw in Mid-Beach, covering three and a half acres. Pick up an espresso at The Market, a modern food bazaar inspired by Spain’s old-word markets, then spend the day by the pool in a private cabana. Relax in the spa's hammam room before dinner at Michelin-rated Chef Jean-Georges Vongerichten’s Spanish-influenced Matador Room. End the night at the Basement (Schrager’s modern-day version of Studio 54) and go ice-skating or bowling in the dramatically lit alley. Or kick back at the Sandbox—a secluded spot just off of the beach—in a hammock with a cold cocktail.

The splurge: Opt for one of the upper-level bungalows and get a roof deck and plunge pool all to yourself.

Rates: Rooms start from $300 per night; bungalows start from $709 per night
Photo: Courtesy of The Miami Beach Edition
4 / 7
Calistoga Ranch

If you live in: San Francisco or Sacramento (about 1 hour and 30 minutes away from either location)

What to do: Sitting on 157 acres in northern California, Calistoga Ranch is the ultimate respite. Soak in the healing waters of the natural mineral pool, or take in the dramatic views of the Mayacamas Mountains from their pool. For the ultimate foodie, The Lakehouse restaurant uses not only organic ingredients from local farmers, but fresh produce from their greenhouse, and private cooking lessons with the ranch's executive chef are available. The resort is also the only of its kind in Napa Valley that has an onsite vineyard. Take advantage of tastings without having to leave the property. And if you're the outdoorsy type, there are 140 hike-able acres.

The splurge: Each cedar-shingled condo has an expansive open-air living space, indoor and outdoor showers (why not bathe under the stars), and floor-to-ceiling windows that provide unencumbered views of the landscape. But if you select the Estate Lodge, you’ll have the option of unwinding by the fireplace in the living room or on the patio, a deck equipped with a hot tub, and unhindered views of Lake Lommel.

Rates: Lodges start from $685 per night; Estate Lodge starts from $4,300 per night
Photo: Courtesy of Calistoga Ranch
5 / 7
Peninsula Chicago

If you live in: Chicago

What to do: Start with a cappuccino at the European-style café Perrot Gourmet in the morning, then try a sparkling wine flight before dining alfresco at the dim sum offerings at Shanghai Terrace (the Peking duck is a must). Take in some rays (or watch the sun set) from the Sun Terrace, swim a few laps in the outdoor pool, then head to the spa. The spa’s massage therapists have just undergone training in Bangkok with the Wat Pho monastery, known as the birthplace of Thai massage.

The splurge: For luxury at its finest, the Peninsula Suite has a private exercise room, fireplace, grand piano, and a 2,000-square-foot furnished wraparound terrace overlooking Lake Michigan with a whirlpool. Suite guests also have the bonus of access to a driver, and if you live within five miles of the hotel, a Mini Cooper will pick you up and drop you off at home.

Rates: Rooms start from $430 per night; Peninsula Suite starts from $9,000 per night
Photo: Courtesy of Peninsula
6 / 7
Park Hyatt Washington

If you live in: Washington D.C.

What to do: Take a dip in the hotel’s indoor, sky-lit saltwater pool or book a spa treatment: everything from a lavender and chamomile mud mask to a hot stone massage. Then sample some of the finest and rarest teas coming from the far reaches of Sri Lanka and the Himalayas at the Tea Cellar, and take a seat at the open-kitchen restaurant, Blue Duck Tavern, which boasts an extensive floor-to-ceiling wine display.

The splurge: Get the Presidential Suite for perks like a private butler’s pantry, a spacious living room with a baby grand piano, along with the luxurious detail of a hand-carved travertine bathtub to fully decompress.

Rates: Rooms start from $199 per night; Presidential Suite starts from $1,999 per night
Photo: Courtesy of Park Hyatt
7 / 7
The Cloister at Sea Island

If you live in: Atlanta (about 4 hours and 45 minutes away), Savannah (about 1 hour and 30 minutes), Jacksonville (about 1 hour and 15 minutes)

What to do: While it may be a bit of a trek from Atlanta, Sea Island is one worth making. Choose from a bevy of outdoor activities, including horseback riding on the beach, sailing, tennis, golfing, kayaking, and fishing, or set your sights on the 65,000-square-foot spa and fitness center for a full day of self-pampering with massages, facials, cycling classes, yoga, and more. And with nine dining establishments on offer, you won’t have to eat at the same place twice.

The splurge: Stay at the Sea Island Suite and take in your surroundings on the private balcony, or cozy up by the fire in the spacious living room.

Rates: Rooms start from $395 per night; Sea Island Suite starts from $1,595 per night
Photo: Courtesy of Sea Island
