New Details on How Homeland Will Radically Re-invent Itself for Season 5

August 11, 2015 6:31 pm
  • Joanna Robinson

Just how different will the dramatically altered fifth season of Homeland be? Well, according to Showtime president David Nevins, we’re in for a whole new story and maybe a radical shake-up is just what the drama needs. Once a critical darling and a ratings hit, the series has sunk in both regards since the departure of co-lead Damian Lewis. But don’t count Carrie Mathison out yet, Nevins says, “there is a lot of life left in Homeland.” According to the new details he spilled at the Television Critics Association’s semi-annual press tour Tuesday, that life is going to be ripped from the headlines.

As we’ve already reported, the fifth season will feature a dramatic two-year time jump, a new job, location, and love interest for Carrie, and a departure from the D.C.-meets-Middle East focus that drove the first four seasons of the show. Instead the action is centered in Berlin because, Nevins says, the German city “is a crossroads between the old Eastern bloc and the Western bloc, a crossroads between Western Europe and the Muslim world. It’s also a place with the strictest privacy laws in the world.” But despite all these changes, there is one thing we can rely upon. Carrie and her famously strange faces? Nevins says they’re “not going anywhere.”

So what will Carrie and her attractive new German lawyer boyfriend (well done, Carrie) get up to in Season 5? Well, like House of Cards before it, Homeland is going after Vladimir Putin. Nevins said, “The first episode’s going to deal with Russia — what’s Putin up to? What’s going on with this tricky relationship there?” And, in a departure from the fictionalized world and show-created terrorists like Haissam Haqqani and Abu Nazir, Homeland will tackle more realistic world events like “ISIS. Charlie Hebdo. [Edward] Snowden, cyber terrorism, and surveillance.”

So, yes, Homeland will look very different next year but there are a few more consistencies we can rely on. A new behind-the-scenes trailer confirms that Mandy Patinkin’s Saul is still very much in the habit of berating Carrie for being her brilliant-yet-stupid self.

And, despite Carrie’s new love interest, we can expect the return of Rupert Friend’s Quinn. (Thank God, Quinn was the highlight of Season 4.) But even Friend will admit that we’re in for something completely different. Earlier this summer he told Deadline:

If I were to hazard a guess, they wanted to do a reboot of everything and rejig the whole concept of the show away from a kind of will they/won’t they Carrie/Brody thing and back into an espionage thriller, and focus on spy craft, which they now seem to be doing pretty much each season really. Certainly this one we’re filming now is very much a do-over, again.
