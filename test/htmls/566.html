Dreamlike Images From A 1963 French Fashion Shoot

In a world where Photoshop transform the impossible into the everyday norm, where Instagram alters HD photographs into filtered 'vintage' low-res versions of themselves, it's easy to forget that once upon a time, REAL photographers existed. More to the point, they had to rely on creativity and ingenuity to create illusionary feats - not a few filters, hidden layers and tilt shifts.

Take iconic photographer Melvin Sokolsky, who back in 1963 was commissioned by fashion magazine Harper’s Bazaar to photograph their “Bubble” Spring Collection.

With world-famous French model Simone D’Aillencourt at his disposal & wearing the most elegant pieces from the new collection, he placed her inside a giant plexiglass bubble, suspending it on a crane across various locations in Paris.

The end result, is one of the most surreal & iconic fashion shoots you will ever see. Even today, 49 years on, it's still cited and referenced as one of the greatest fashion shoots of all time.

An amazing feat considering the kind of chaos it would have caused during rush hour, but even more impressively the chords that held the bubble in place are completely invisible. How did he do it? Only Melvin Sokolsky know the secret, but one thing we do know for sure - back in those days, photography was a genuine artform that few attempted and even less could truly master.
