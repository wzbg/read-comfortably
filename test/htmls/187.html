Incredible Dirty Car Art by Scott Wade

by jusone in Inspirations - follow us on Facebook:

San Antonio, Texas based artist Scott Wade is always experimenting new ways to express his artistic views and known for his popular pieces of Dirty Car Art. He created variety of artistic drawings on the canvas of car windows with his fingers using limestone and clay. The original drawings will not last long but each piece was photographed and images are really stunning! Let enjoy it.


FAST FOOD SUCKS
This piece was created as a “permanent” gallery piece in support of the Texas Breastfeeding Coalition’s legislative efforts. To be shown as part of a collection of breastfeeding-related artworks in the Texas State Capitol, from February 16th through the 23rd, and to tour as a collection in the future to raise awareness of issues relating to mothers’ rights to breastfeed their children in public, and to be able to pump breast milk in the workplace, among other issues. One of very few pieces of “permanent” Dirty Car Art. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


STEVEN AND ALBERT
Piece created for the San Marcos Public Library, January 2015. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


WOLF AND TROJAN
I was invited to the University of Western Georgia to give an artist talk and create a pice for their Environmental Awareness Week. I was also asked to do this piece for the local high school’s homecoming game. The Wolf represents the university, while the Trojan is the mascot for the the high school. The Trojans won, BTW. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


MAY THE FORZA BE WITH YOU
I’ve worked on a bunch of different models of vehicles, but this is my first Lamborghini! Created this piece for Microsoft Studio’s release of the new Forza Horizon II Xbox One game. They created a video of me working on it, but I don’t think it ever saw the light of day. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


FLOWER POWER
Flo commissioned me to create this portrait of her. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


LUNCHEON OF THE BOATING PARTY
This detail from Renoir’s excellent painting was created for the Wine & Art Walk in San Marcos, TX. 3/7/2014. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


A FRIEND IN NEED
From the famous painting by C.M. Coolidge. I created this one right after Mona Lisa / Starry Night. It was suggested to me by my friend, John McDavitt. He’s had more than one good idea for me! (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


AQUARIUM #1
This piece was created at the Art Splash in Carlsbad, CA. The idea was to visually turn the interior of the car into an aquarium, featuring sea life from the California coast. September, 2009. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)



RELATIVITY
From the famous work of M.C. Escher. Created as part of a gallery exhibition for Carstar’s annual “Soaps It Up” fundraiser. Toronto, June, 2012. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


STAGECOACH
From a promotional image for the John Wayne movie. This one was created for an art festival in Killeen, Texas, Take 190 West. March, 2013. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


HYLAS AND THE NYMPHS
From Waterhouse’s romantic painting. Created at the Seattle Art Car Blowout during The Freemont Fair. What a great time we had there! Those Seattle art car folks are a great bunch of folks. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


AMERICAN GOTHIC
After the famous painting by American master, Grant Wood. Created for Inside Edition. Unfortunately, this episode never made it to the air. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


HOUSTON BLUES
Great blues musicians that called Houston home: Albert Collins, Lightnin’ Hopkins, Big Mama Thornton, and Clarence “Gate Mouth” Brown. Created for Via Colori, sponsored by Houston MINI Dealerships, Houston, TX 2012. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


DESERT DUST
Here’s another piece I did for the cancelled Ford Escape ad campaign. The complaint was it looked too much like a photograph, so the viewers would just think it was Photoshopped. I tried to tell them to make a commercial with it instead, but marketing types never listen. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)



POLAR BEARS (IN A DUST STORM?)
Created as part of the gallery show for Carstar’s annual “Soaps It Up” fundraiser, in Toronto. Toronto, June 2012. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


SCOTT & WILLIE
Another window in the Willie Nelson series I did at Maker Fair, Austin, TX. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


GIRL WITH A PEARL EARRING
An early dusty homage to Vermeer’s classic masterpiece. The “natural” canvas had been used before, and covered over by more dust. It was very crusty, so I had to stab at the dirt with a brush, which gives it its interesting texture. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


TEXAS WILDFLOWERS
Created for a South Korean Television show, shot in Cuero, TX. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


ATLANTA #1
I was invited to do some work at the Atlanta Arts Festival. My friend, John McDavitt, had this killer idea to do a family as if they were on vacation inside the vehicle. September, 2008. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)


MY OL’ PAL DRIFTER
Greatest dog friend I’ve ever had. Here’s to you, buddy. RIP. (Image and captions by Scott Wade)
