Go wine tasting in the winter and wait until September to visit summer hot spots: Infographic reveals best off-season travel times and destinations

By Katie Amey for MailOnline 11:04 09 Aug 2015, updated 12:25 09 Aug 2015

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • 161 shares
  • 74 comments
  • Popular destinations may be far more affordable during off-peak months
  • Travelling in the off-season means fewer crowds and discounted hotels
  • Skiers on a budget should wait until early spring when slopes are quieter
  • September is a great time to score lower rates at popular summer spots

During the height of summer, costs can easily skyrocket when planning a much-needed getaway.

But for those who are eager to jet off - and remain budget-conscious - this useful infographic highlights the most affordable destinations anytime of year.

Travelling during the off-season offers tourists plenty of benefits: fewer crowds, discounted hotel prices and quieter restaurants.

Scroll down for video

Ski bunnies should hit the slopes in early spring in order to get the best bang for their buck - the snow is still great quality in the higher altitudes, but there aren't as many people around. Consider places like the Rocky Mountains and Denver, Colorado.

Waiting until September, which is known as 'the shoulder season,' is a great time to score lower rates at popular summer destinations.

If you can only get the summer months off for a vacation, it may be wise to look further afield.

Tropical beaches are typically cheaper in the summer because not only is there a great deal of competition with the seasonal local beaches, but also because hurricane season tends to hit at this time in places like the Caribbean and Thailand, Marriott's money-saving infographic outlines.

Finally, the winter months are actually a great time to explore wine country, such as Napa and Santa Barbara, California, when the vineyards won't be as crowded.
